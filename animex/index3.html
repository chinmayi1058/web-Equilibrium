Graphics Design

Students learn the fundamentals of two-dimensional design, the foundation of art, graphic design, and visual communication. This unit focuses on how through simple, yet intentional visual manipulation, points, lines, planes, gestalt, and color can communicate and create meaning.

What we are doing

The game to aim, game development club provides a collaborative experience for like-minded individuals to work together, discover interests, and develop innovative games using new technologies. We provide members with the training, tools, and guidance to complete their projects. The club provides an outlet for students to socialize and learn from their peers, develop job skills, and participate in other campus activities. We facilitate the creation of projects that members can add to their portfolios, which, through the skills, experience, and contacts they gain, will give them an edge when entering the career they wish to pursue. Our club promotes a safe, nurturing environment for students looking to become game developers. We encourage not only personal growth in the field but provide a collaborative environment to learn to work as a team. The Game Developers Club has been active in designing and creating games, promoting gaming in the community, and working with other organizations, and participating in various game jams. We have members from a wide range of fields such as designers, video editors, animators, coders, sound engineers, and scriptwriters.

Game programer

Essentially, a game programmer writes the code that brings a video game to life. They work closely with producers and other departments to translate the project's vision into a fully functioning, playable game.

Game designer

A game designer conceptualizes game plots and storylines, levels and environments, character interactions, and other creative aspects. It's their job to work with other specialists - like developers, artists, and others .

Game Publisher

A video game publisher is a company that publishes video games that have been developed either internally by the publisher or externally by a video game developer.
